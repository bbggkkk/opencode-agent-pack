# Style Guide

## Style Contract

- Prose baseline: elegant, controlled, and assured literary prose by a renowned, seasoned professional novelist.
- Language: Korean.
- Cultural background: contemporary Korean speculative thriller.
- Genre register: restrained, tense, observant, never slapstick.
- Sentence rhythm: measured medium-length sentences with short turns at moments of threat.
- Diction: concrete sensory nouns, clean verbs, no internet slang.
- Metaphor density: low; metaphors must clarify pressure or memory.
- POV distance: close third-person, anchored to Han Seo-yun.
- POV person: third-person narration.
- Tense: past tense narration except direct dialogue and immediate sensory fragments.
- Viewpoint anchor: Han Seo-yun's perceptions, knowledge, and pressure lead the scene.
- Head-hopping rule: no unmarked interiority from I-an, Director Chae, or unseen observers.
- Emotional temperature: controlled surface, visible pressure through gesture.
- Dialogue texture: compressed, purposeful, subtext-heavy.

## Drift Guards

- Required Style Anchors: close third-person pressure, restrained threat, precise sensory nouns, controlled emotional surface, subtext-heavy dialogue.
- Forbidden Style Drift: internet slang, slapstick relief, melodramatic exposition, sudden omniscient explanation, florid metaphor chains, direct imitation of living authors.
- Forbidden Literal Phrases: 레전드급, 개웃기다, 실화냐.
- Style Verification Questions: Does the prose stay close to Seo-yun's perception? Does each metaphor clarify pressure or memory? Does dialogue carry subtext without explaining the emotion?
- Revision Guardrails: edits may tighten rhythm and clarify sensory action, but must not add jokes, broaden POV, raise metaphor density, or change character register without verified cause.
- Do not break the controlled literary baseline for comedic relief.
- Do not add slang unless a character sheet explicitly allows it.
- Do not explain feelings when a gesture or choice can carry them.
- Do not directly imitate living authors; convert references into broad traits.
